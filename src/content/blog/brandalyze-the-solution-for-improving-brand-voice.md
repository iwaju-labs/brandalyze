If you've ever read your own website, blog, social posts and emails and thought, *"Do these even sound like the same brand?"* or something to that effect, then you're not alone.

Brands that are getting started, and brands scaling out their content across platforms are finding maintaining a consistent brand voice harder than ever. Add AI-generated content into the mix, and the problem only gets worse. Sure content gets faster, but identity can be lost.

That's what **Brandalyze** was built to solve.

## What is Brand Voice?
Brand voice (or brand identity) is the personality behind your words. It's how your brand sounds &mdash; whether that's professional/casual, confident, humorous, etc. A good example of this is McDonald's. McDonald's has well established it's brand voice to be approachable, and authentic.

While tone can change a lot based on context, e.g. a launch compared to a support email, your brand voice should remain consistent everywhere your brand presents itself.

When it doesn't, people will notice &mdash; even if they can't explain why.

## Why Inconsistent Brand Voice is a Problem!
Inconsistent brand voice is not just a nitpick. It directly affects how people perceive and trust your product.


When your messaging feels disjointed:
- Users can struggle to recognize your brand
- Trust can erode over time
- Engagement drops
- Content creation slows down because scripting will take more time
And today, with AI tools generating content at scale, inconsistency can multiply fast.

## Why Existing Solutions Fall Short
Most brands rely on style guides, Notion docs, or internal examples to define their voice. But these approaches are static, easy to ignore, and rarely enforced.

On the other hand AI writing tools are great at generating content &mdash; *just not for your brand*. Without guidance, they default to generic, robotic sounding output.
What's missing is a way to efficiently analyze brand voice itself &mdash; not just generate text.
